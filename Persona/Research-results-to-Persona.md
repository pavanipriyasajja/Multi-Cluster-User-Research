## User research survey results to Hybrid Engineer persona generations and journey mapping

### What drove the decision to develop personas and journey maps?

Even after collecting multi cluster survey research raw data and summarizing results, the information can still feel scattered and difficult for us to apply directly in the multi cluster solutions. 

The research raw data explains what engineers said and what problems exist, but it does not always make it clear how those problems affect different types of engineers or when exactly they occur in real-life situations.

By using the user research methods such as Creating personas and journey maps helps organize this information in a way that is easier to understand and use.

Personas can be thought of as simplified representations of different types of users. Instead of looking at many individual responses, similar behaviors and challenges are grouped together into one “type” of user. This makes it easier for teams to think about real people rather than abstract data. For example, instead of saying “some users struggle with deployment,” the team can refer to a specific type of user who regularly faces that challenge and understand their situation more clearly. 

Journey mapping (In engineering terms workflow mapping), on the other hand, shows the step-by-step experience of that engineer. Rather than looking at problems in isolation, it connects them into a sequence. This helps identify when issues happen—for example, whether a problem occurs at the beginning of a task, during execution, or at the final stage. Seeing the full flow makes it easier to understand how one issue can lead to another.

This step is important because it helps teams focus on what matters most. Research often reveals many different problems, but not all of them are equally important. By organizing the information into user types and their experiences, it becomes clearer which problems have the biggest impact and should be addressed first.

It also helps different teams stay aligned. When people from different backgrounds such as engineering, product, or management look at raw data, they may interpret it differently. Personas and journey maps provide a shared understanding, so everyone is working with the same picture of the user and their challenges.

Most importantly, this process helps turn research into action. Without it, research can remain as a collection of findings without clear direction. By clearly defining who the users are and how they experience a process, teams can more easily decide what improvements to make and where to focus their efforts.

In simple terms, research results tell what is happening, while personas and journey maps explain who it is happening to and how it happens step by step. This makes it much easier to make informed and effective decisions.
